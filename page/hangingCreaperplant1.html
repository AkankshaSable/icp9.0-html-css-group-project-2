They are also referred to as procumbent plants.1 Some of the fastest-growing creeping plants include creeping Jenny, evening primrose, periwinkle, wintercreeper, English ivy, sweet woodruff, blue star creeper, and bugleweed. In this sense, creepers differ from "climbers," which are vines that tend to attach to structures on their own.
